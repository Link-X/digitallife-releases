What it does

Understand your disk,
one ring at a time.

Point DiskLite at any folder or drive you allow, and it turns raw file sizes into something you can actually read.

Sunburst space map

A ring-by-ring view of what fills your disk. Click any wedge to drill into that folder; hover to see its path and size.

Breakdown by type

See how much space video, images, documents, archives and installers each take — the whole disk, at a glance.

Big files, duplicates & old files

Four ranked lists — the largest files, byte-for-byte duplicates, leftover installers, and big files untouched for 180+ days — so you know exactly what's reclaimable.

Cleans to the Trash — only

Sweep app caches, browser caches, logs and developer junk — even uninstall apps together with their leftovers. Everything goes to the Trash with your confirmation, always reversible.

Private by design

Runs entirely inside the Apple sandbox. Nothing is uploaded, nothing is collected — your files never leave your Mac.

Native & 8 languages

Built with SwiftUI for Apple Silicon, light on resources, and localized into 8 languages that follow your system.

The sunburst

A map, not a spreadsheet.

Folder sizes are hard to feel as numbers. As nested rings, the space hog is obvious in a second.

  • Proportional rings. Each wedge's angle is its share of the disk — the biggest folders jump out instantly.
  • Click to drill down. Follow the space inward, folder by folder, then breadcrumb your way back out.
  • Tiny folders merged. Small entries fold into one "other" slice, so the picture stays clean, never cluttered.
Privacy by design

Your files never leave your Mac.

There's no server to send anything to — because DiskLite never talks to one.

  • 100% local. Every scan is processed on your own Mac. No file, name, path or result is ever uploaded.
  • No account, no network. DiskLite works fully offline. No sign-in, no tracking, no analytics SDKs.
  • You grant, you control. It only touches the folders you grant — and cleanup never deletes outright, it only moves items to the Trash.
